I know Singapore aims to be an AI Hub...but should we let it cook?

6 June 2026

Singapore has announced ambitions to become a regional AI hub, with plans to build data centres across the island. As someone who studied ecocriticism alongside literature and history, I find myself asking questions that the tech press doesn't always raise: what does this mean for an already hot, water-scarce city-state in a warming world?

Data centres are notoriously thirsty and heat-intensive. In a tropical climate like Singapore's, cooling them is not a trivial problem — and the concern compounds when you factor in urban heat islands and climate change. Diverting water resources to cool servers cannot come at the cost of the people who live here.

I lack the technical expertise to solve this, so I did what I usually do — I asked my AI friends. A few ideas came up that seem worth taking seriously: seawater cooling (we are, after all, an island), underground data centres to reduce surface heat load, and stricter PUE efficiency standards as a condition of any permit granted. I can't verify all of this independently, but I think these directions deserve proper expert scrutiny.

Singapore cannot afford to be left behind in AI, and I understand that. But being an AI hub and being a liveable, sustainable city are not mutually exclusive goals — unless we let them become so by not asking the harder questions early enough.
