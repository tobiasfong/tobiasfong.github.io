About the Blog

Adeptus Mechanicus Knight is a hobby blog dedicated to delving into the deep lore, narrative history, and faction evolution of Warhammer 40,000 — the long-running tabletop miniature wargame published by Games Workshop. Across its decades of existence, the 40K universe has grown into one of the most richly detailed science-fantasy settings ever conceived, and this blog is an attempt to archive and explore that legacy.

What You'll Find

  • Faction histories — how armies like the Adeptus Mechanicus, Imperial Knights, Astra Militarum, and the Collegia Titanica have evolved in lore and rules across editions
  • Narrative deep dives — examining the storylines, campaigns, and events that have shaped the setting over the decades
  • Edition retrospectives — looking back at how the game's tone, aesthetics, and world-building have shifted from Rogue Trader through to modern 40K
  • Archival research — digging into old White Dwarf issues, Codexes, and out-of-print sourcebooks to trace the origins of beloved factions and characters

Why Warhammer 40,000?

The 40K setting is unique in that its lore has been built collaboratively by hundreds of writers, artists, and game designers over nearly four decades — resulting in a universe full of contradictions, retcons, and unexpected depth. Tracing how a faction like the Adeptus Mechanicus went from a mysterious background detail in the original Rogue Trader rulebook to a fully realised army with its own rich mythology is the kind of historical puzzle this blog loves to unpack.

Whether you're a veteran hobbyist or just curious about one of the most expansive fictional universes in gaming, there's something here for you.
